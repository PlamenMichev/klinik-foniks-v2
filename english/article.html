How tea can change your life and how to prepare it

What is the first thing you think about when you catch the obligatory annual cold? Tea with honey.

Tea originated from the far East, where it was initially discovered by the Eas India Company in the 17th century. They brought it with them home, and soon the whole West was fascinated by this infused drink - and they’re well within their right to be. Tea has a long history of being used as a healing remedy, and the vast amount of different herbs makes it useful for pretty much anything. Here’s a list of the most popular types of tea, what temperature water to use and how long to steep them for:

  • Black, 100 C, 3-4 min
  • Green, 80 C, 2-3 min
  • White, 70 C, 1-2 min
  • Oolong, 80 C, 3-4 min
  • Rooibos, 100 C, 4-5 min

You may think “What does it matter? I always just use boiling water and keep the tea bag in!” - and taste is one thing, but here’s the problem: if you, for example, steep the tea in water that is too hot, it will dismantle the complex nutrients in the tea, so you won’t get the most amount of health benefits out of it.

Depending on the tea, it can help fight curable everyday problems like bloating, detoxifying, blood circulation, stress, anxiety, insomnia and much more. They all serve their own purposes, and sometimes tea bag packets also state what their health benefits are. One thing you have to look out for when you’re buying bags, that the flavor is not just from added aromas. The best kind of teas are the ones without flavoring, so look for bags with a longer ingredient list. This is where loose tea comes in, as it is usually higher in quality and you have a direct visualization of what’s in the tea.

Start drinking tea conscious of the individual health benefits today, and watch your life change for the better!
